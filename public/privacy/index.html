Tiny Next Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026

Tiny Next is a small Android task picker that helps users choose one manageable next task. This policy explains what the app stores, what third-party services may process, and how to contact the developer.

Information Tiny Next Stores

Tiny Next is designed to work without an account. Task data is expected to stay on the user's device unless a future version clearly adds sync, export, support upload, or another feature that moves data away from the device.

The app may store local app preferences, task content, progress state, completion history, and the local cache of whether the one-time premium purchase has been restored or confirmed.

Advertising

The free version may show banner ads through Google AdMob. Google Mobile Ads may process device identifiers, approximate location, app interaction data, diagnostics, and advertising-related signals according to Google's SDK behavior and policies.

Tiny Next does not include interstitial ads or rewarded ads in this MVP.

In-App Purchases

Tiny Next offers a one-time Google Play Billing product named remove_ads_premium. Google Play processes purchases. Tiny Next stores only the local entitlement state needed to hide ads and restore premium access.

Data Sharing

Tiny Next does not sell personal data. Data may be processed by Google services used for ads, Play Billing, app distribution, platform security, and crash diagnostics if those platform diagnostics are enabled by Google Play.

Retention and Deletion

Task data remains on the user's device until the user edits it, deletes it, archives it, clears app storage, uninstalls Tiny Next, or uses the in-app "Delete all local data" action.

Children

Tiny Next is intended for a general productivity audience and is not directed at children.
